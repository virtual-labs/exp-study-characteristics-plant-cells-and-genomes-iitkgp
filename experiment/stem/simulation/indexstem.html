Instructions
  1. A fresh stem of a plant (Amaranthus) is chosen.
  2. Click on the blade to cut a small portion of stem.
  3. Stem section is placed onto a glass microscope slide.
  4. Click on the forcep. Click on the blade to cut a small sectiom of stem.
  5. Click on the forcep to remove the excess stem.
  6. Click on the brush to pick the stem section. Click on the brush to move the stem cut onto a petridish. Click on the brush to move the brush to its original place.
  7. Click on the dropper to draw Safranin solution from the beaker. Click to move the dropper above the petridish. Click to drop a solution of safranin for 2-5 minutes.
  8. Click on the brush to pick the stem section. Click on brush to move the stem cut onto a petridish. Click on the brush to move the brush to its original place.
  9. Click on the dropper to draw distilled water from the beaker. Click to move the dropper above the petridish. Click to drop distilled water.
  10. Click on the brush to pick the stem section. Click on brush to move the stem cut onto a glass slide. Click on the brush to move the brush the brush to its original place.
  11. Click on the dropper to draw water from the beaker. Click to move the dropper above the glass slide. Click to drop water.
  12. Click on the coverslip to place over the stem section.
  13. Place the prepared clean slide on the stage of the microscope.
  14. Start with the magnification objective lens, to view the cross section of the stem.
Equipement Required
